Removing Stop Words

counter = Counter()
for token in document:
    if not token.is_stop and not token.pos_ in unwanted:
        counter[token.lemma_] += 1

for token, count in counter.most_common()[:10]:
    print(f" - {token}: {count}")

I guess pronouns are stop words… It looks like "Verloc" is now the most common token. I assume he's the main character in the book. According to the WikiPedia article about The Secret Agent -

The story is set in London in 1886 and deals with Mr Anton Verloc and his work as a spy for an unnamed country (presumably Russia).

End

The idea behind this was to show that word counts follow Zipf's Law, which I wasn't able to do. If it was following Zipf's law then we would expect the log-log plots to turn into straight lines.
